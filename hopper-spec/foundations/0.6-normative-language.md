## Normative Language

This specification uses the following terms as defined in RFC 2119.

**MUST** — An absolute requirement. A conforming implementation must satisfy this requirement without exception.

**MUST NOT** — An absolute prohibition. A conforming implementation must never do this.

**SHALL** — Equivalent to MUST.

**SHALL NOT** — Equivalent to MUST NOT.

**SHOULD** — A strong recommendation. Valid reasons may exist to deviate, but the implications must be understood and carefully weighed before choosing a different course.

**SHOULD NOT** — A strong recommendation against. Valid reasons may exist, but the implications must be understood.

**MAY** — Optional. A conforming implementation may or may not do this at its discretion.

---

Additionally, this specification uses the following conventions:

**Implementation-defined.** The behavior is not specified here but must be documented by the implementation. Programs that depend on implementation-defined behavior are not portable, but they are not undefined.

**Unspecified.** The behavior is not specified. A conforming implementation may do anything, but unlike undefined behavior, the implementation is not required to document what it does.

**Undefined.** The behavior is not defined. Programs that trigger undefined behavior are not conforming. An implementation may assume undefined behavior does not occur and optimize accordingly.
